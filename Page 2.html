my gaming journey

player xp

I first started playing games at the young age of 6. I did not have a game console, PC, or even a mobile phone back then. All I could do was watch my cousins play and always ask them if I could borrow their PSP. Even if I only got a few minutes to play, it felt like the best thing ever. As time went on, I started learning more just by watching. I would pay attention to how they moved in games, how they completed levels, and how they handled challenges. I didn’t realize it at the time, but I was slowly building my understanding of gaming. My favorite game at that time was GTA: Liberty City. I was fascinated by the open world, the freedom to explore, and just how alive everything felt. Even just watching it being played made me excited every time.

Then after a few years, my dad bought me an iPod Touch, which marked the start of my mobile gaming era. That’s when I really got into gaming on my own. I played the OG classics like Temple Run, Flappy Bird, Jetpack Joyride, Fruit Ninja, and many more. I would spend hours trying to beat my high scores and compete with friends. My favorite mobile game at that time was Inotia 4: Assassin of Berkel.

Eventually, I got my first console, an Xbox 360. In my opinion, it is the best gaming console I’ve ever owned because of the memories, games, and experiences it gave me. It opened up a whole new world of gaming for me, from story-driven adventures to competitive multiplayer, and made gaming feel even more real and exciting. My favorite games on the Xbox 360 were Halo 4 and Need for Speed: Most Wanted. I used to play them a lot with my brother and cousins.

As I got older, I went back to mobile gaming because it was more accessible and I could play anywhere. I started playing Mobile Legends, which was the first competitive game I ever got into. I adapted to it quickly and eventually found a squad. We would join tournaments, but we always ended up losing. After a few months, my first squadmates slowly quit the game, and I was the only one left. I felt sad not having anyone to play with. After grinding solo queue for a while, someone invited me to join their squad. I joined and fit in quickly. Our chemistry was strong, and this time, we started winning tournaments. My rank started to rise, and I eventually peaked at 2000 points during that time.
